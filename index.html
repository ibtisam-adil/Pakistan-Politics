PAKISTAN'S POLITICS
FROM 1947 TO 2023.

Pakistan gained independence from British colonial rule in 1947 after the partition of India. It has since faced political and social challenges, but has made progress in areas such as education and infrastructure development.

Time Periods

1958

Pakistan's early years were unstable, led by Muslim League's Jinnah until his death. Weak civilian govts followed until 1958.

1971

Ayub Khan's coup in 1958 granted him vast powers, dissolved the National Assembly. War with India in 1965.

1977

Bangladesh declared independence in 1971, won civil war, became independent. 1973 constitution established Pakistan as republic.

1988

Zia-ul-Haq took power in 1977, imposed martial law and Islamic laws, curtailed civil liberties. He died in 1988.

2008

Musharraf's rule saw economic and social reforms, better ties with West, but marred by corruption, abuses. Declared emergency in 2007, resigned in 2008.
